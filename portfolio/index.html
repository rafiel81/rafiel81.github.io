About me

I am a sculptor shaping my masterpiece: the heart.

If love is love no matter what, then my mission is clear: to use media and events as platforms that honor, amplify, and celebrate every kind of love — loudly, proudly, and unapologetically.

My journey to RMIT has been shaped by five projects and clubs that mirror the five love languages — each teaching me to celebrate connection through Multimedia Communication, especially in wedding planning. These experiences from multiple scopes of work refined my belief in love as a universal language, one that transcends tradition and embraces all forms of commitment.

The stone and lavender visual anchors my philosophy: love, especially queer love, grows not in ideal conditions, but through pressure, tradition, and fracture, where tenderness insists on blooming.

Now, I aspire to revolutionize wedding and anniversary storytelling, creating spaces where LGBTQIA+ love is honored with the same artistry as traditional unions. With RMIT’s global lens and my communication skills, I’ll craft narratives that don’t just document love, but actively redefine its celebration.
